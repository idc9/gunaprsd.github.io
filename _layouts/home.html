Research Interests
I am interested in developing and analyzing statistical algorithms for non-standard data such as: networks, text and high-dimensional data. My research draws on probability, optimization, and statistics/machine learning and is currently driven by problems from neuroscience and the law.
  • Support Vector Machine behavior in high-dimensional settings
  • Large scale network and text analysis of the legal citaion network
  • Dimensionality reduction for network valued data
Brief Bio
I am a graduate student in Statistics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill where I am co-advised by Shankar Bhamidi and J.S Marron. Before coming to North Carolina I stuided math and physics at Cornell University. At Cornell I worked on theCuSat engineering project team and in the a bio-phyiscs lab led by Carl Franck.

In addition to my research, I believe in making data science and the ability to reason about data more accessible to people without an advanced degree or even a traditional STEM background. This means teaching a practical focused, combination of programming (R/Python), statistics, and communication skills. With funding from Data@Carolina and alot of help I developed STOR 390: Introduction to Data Science.
