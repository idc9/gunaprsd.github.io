Research Interests
I am interested in developing and analyzing statistical algorithms for non-standard data such as: networks, text, images, and multi-view data. My research draws on stochastic processes, high dimensional statistics, deep learning, and optimization. The scientific questions driving my work come from:the law, natural language processing, genetics, histopathology and medical imaging.


Brief Bio
I will be starting as an NSF Mathematical Sciences Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of Washington, hosted by Daniela Witten in the summer of 2019. I am currently finishing my PhD in Statistics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill where I am advised by Shankar Bhamidi and J.S Marron. Before coming to North Carolina I majored in math and physics at Cornell University.

I am committed to making modern, computational methodologies more accessible to statisticians and scientists, as well as those without a traditional STEM background. With funding from Data@Carolina (and a lot of help) I created and taught the first data science course offered by UNC's statistics department (STOR 390: Introduction to Data Science) which is now a permanent part of the curriculum.
